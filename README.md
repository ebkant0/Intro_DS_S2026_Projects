## Final Project – Behind the Paycheck: Salary Patterns in DC Public Employment

Analysis of 40,611 DC public employee compensation records sourced from DC Open Data. The project explores which factors — job title, agency, appointment type, grade level, and seniority — are most strongly associated with higher compensation rates in Washington, DC government.

**Key Findings:**
- Job title is the strongest predictor — medical officers average $280K–$350K
- Executive appointments earn 2.3× more than civil service appointments
- Small specialized agencies consistently outpay large agencies
- Grade level (r=0.48) predicts compensation far better than years of service (r=0.12)

## Project 2 – Income Analysis Using the Adult Census Dataset

Group project analyzing 1994 US Census Bureau data to determine which factors are most associated with earning over $50,000 annually. The dataset includes demographic and employment variables such as age, education, occupation, workclass, and native country.

**Key Findings:**
- Education showed the clearest relationship with income — higher education levels appeared more frequently in the over $50K group
- Workclass type was associated with stronger earning potential
- Age showed a noticeable pattern — older individuals appeared more often in the higher income category
